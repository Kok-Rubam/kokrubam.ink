About

kokrubam (kok rubam) is the Kokborok word for "dictionary" — from kok (word, language) + rubam (collection, arrangement).

The Dictionary

This is a digitized version of the comprehensive Kokborok-English Dictionary authored by Bishnu Charan Debbarma. The original dictionary was written using BanglaWord, a pre-Unicode word processor, and stored across 67 RTF files.

The dictionary contains over 9,800 entries covering Kokborok vocabulary with English definitions, parts of speech, pronunciation guides, cross-references, and derived forms.

Kokborok Language

Kokborok (also called Tripuri) is a Tibeto-Burman language spoken by approximately 1 million people, primarily in the state of Tripura in northeast India. Its ISO 639-3 code is trp.

The name "Kokborok" itself means "language of the people" — kok (language) + borok (people, human).
